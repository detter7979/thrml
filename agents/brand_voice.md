# thrml — Brand Voice Guide

## Brand Essence
thrml is a peer-to-peer wellness space marketplace. We connect people who own
private saunas, hot tubs, and cold plunges with guests who want to access them
by the hour — without the commitment of a gym membership or spa booking.

## Tone Pillars
1. **Quiet confidence** — We don't hype. We state facts that sell themselves.
   BAD: "INCREDIBLE private sauna experience!!"
   GOOD: "Private sauna. No membership. Book by the hour."

2. **Lived-in warmth** — Our spaces are real homes, not hotel amenities.
   The host's cedar barrel sauna has been rained on. The towels are good but
   not monogrammed. That authenticity is the product.

3. **Aspirational but attainable** — This isn't a luxury splurge. It's a
   $40 Tuesday evening ritual you can actually maintain.

4. **Data-respecting** — Guests book because they've seen results (recovery,
   sleep, stress). We name the outcome, not the feeling.

## Host Voice (Acquisition)
- Anchors on idle asset + income opportunity
- Never guilt-trips. Frame as unlocking value that already exists.
- Key phrases: "already there," "sitting unused," "your schedule"
- Avoid: "passive income," "side hustle," "easy money"

## Guest Voice (Conversion)
- Anchors on access, not ownership
- Key phrases: "book by the hour," "private," "no membership"
- Outcome language: "recover faster," "sleep better," "decompress"
- Avoid: "luxury," "exclusive," "indulge," "treat yourself"

## Copy Rules
- Headlines: ≤6 words, sentence case, no punctuation unless a question
- Body: Short paragraphs. One idea per sentence.
- CTA: Active verb + specific noun. "Book a sauna" not "Learn more"
- Never use emojis in ad copy
- Never use "wellness journey" or "self-care"
